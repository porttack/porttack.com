Good evening. My name is Eric Brown. I am a member of <a href="https://santacruzhostlionsclub.org/">Santa Cruz Lions</a>.

If you're not familiar with Lions, we're an international service organization -- like [Rotary](https://slvrotary.org/), [Kiwanis](https://svkiwanis.org/), [Omega Nu](https://www.duckyderbysantacruz.org/) or [Valley Womens](https://valleywomensclub.org/) -- focused on improving the lives of people in our communities. Worldwide there are over a million Lions members. Here in Santa Cruz County, our club supports youth in many ways.

<!--more-->

Lions has deep roots in this valley. At one of our home games a few months back, I was talking to an old timer, a one-time captain of the SLV football team decades and decades ago. He told me how the floods of the late 1950s destroyed the school's softball fields that used to live at the entrance to Henry Cowell Park -- just to the right as you go in. It was, at the time, SLV Lions who came together after the floods to build the current softball fields that are part of today's High School.

President Jimmy Carter once said:

> "Lions changed my life. I would have never been ambitious enough to run for Governor of Georgia. I would have never dreamed of being President of the United States if it were not for the Lions."

Service shapes people. It builds character, opens up the world, and sometimes changes everything.

Our scholarship recipient is selected by the school's counselors -- and this year, they chose someone I'm proud to say I know personally.

When COVID closed schools and cut kids off from one another, our recipient didn't wait for someone else to step up. He started a program for older students to read to elementary kids because we all need stories -- and we all need connection. And that wasn't a one-time thing -- throughout his 12 years with SLV schools, he has shown up, again and again, to give his time to others.

That is exactly the spirit Lions was built on, and exactly why we're proud to honor him tonight.

Adam Harrell, please come up.

*Delivered 5/28/2026, SLV Senior Awards Night*
