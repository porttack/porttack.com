I'm the incoming president of the <a href="https://portal.clubrunner.ca//6779">SLV Rotary Club</a>. And Rotary's shared value of service to others is part of why I became a teacher. In fact, it was some long conversations with Andrew at Rotary meetings that helped me make my decision to become a teacher.

You probably know <a href="https://portal.clubrunner.ca//6779">SLV Rotary</a> as the Vax the Valley people -- we ran those COVID clinics in Felton and Ben Lomond, giving out 1000s of vaccines. In the past, we also put dictionaries in the hands of every 4th grader. In fact, my kids got one (though I think they mostly looked up bad words and then lost them under the beds).

<!--more-->

[Rotary International](https://www.rotary.org) itself is over 1.3 million rotarians in 45,000 clubs in nearly every country of the world, trying to make the world a better place. Since 1988, for example, Rotary International has [virtually eliminated polio](https://www.rotary.org/en/our-causes/ending-polio), taking the world from 350,000 cases in 125 countries in 1988 to 40 cases in 2025. Last year, I [traveled to Guatemala to install clean water systems](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1lDz4XiRvGh2GLMPMPwx0JUFJKESKDOkjRwV5HbFbVQQ/edit?usp=sharing) in a community health center and a rural public school. Again, trying to make the world a better place.

Rotary's motto is "service above self". Looking around this room, I think the people being honored tonight already live that.

So for retiring teachers, <a href="https://portal.clubrunner.ca//6779">SLV Rotary</a> has an offer: If you're interested, we'd like to sponsor up to two retiring teachers in their first year of Rotary membership at any club in Santa Cruz County - SLV (my club), Santa Cruz, Sunrise, Capitola-Aptos, wherever you land. We'll cover dues up to $300. The only ask is your time: a couple of visits and a conversation before you join. My hope is that it would be an opportunity to meet new friends who share similar values.

I'm leaving my card. If you're curious, reach out.

Eric Brown
