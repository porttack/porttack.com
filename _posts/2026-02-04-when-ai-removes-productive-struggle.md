You all know me. I've been in this room on and off for 12 years - sometimes giving public comment. I've worn many hats in this community to support it at large and, frequently, our youth.

What you may not know is that I'm an Electrical Engineer. Computer Science classes were what I did for fun. In 1989, I took a graduate-level artificial intelligence class focused on neural networks. Some things have changed with neural nets, but the biggest is scale. They're what is at the heart of modern LLMs. In my long career in silicon valley, I spent 10 years developing machine learning systems to fight spam. I'm still an active computer scientist, and I do use modern AI tools in my own work.

<!--more-->

I also researched and purchased [digital citizenship curriculum](/2020/01/01/cybercivics.html) for this district when my children entered middle school. Before covid I taught some of these. I brought the [Screenagers movie](/2024/05/01/screenagers-next-chapter.html) about youth and social media to two different high schools in our county. I'm showing it again to my 9th graders in a few weeks in our High School wellness center.

And while I've only been teaching for 3-years, I have worked with youth in our schools since about 2017 -- creating, funding, and running enrichment programs.

I have a good perspective on current technology. I have some knowledge of childhood development. And I have watched, firsthand in my own classrooms for the past two years, **what happens when AI removes the productive struggle that students actually need to learn. That is my concern.**

Regarding the AI Task Force recommendations, I want to give you some notice: I may not be able to endorse them. I will elaborate if that happens. As always, I will continue to try to be collaborative.

*Delivered 2/4/2026 6:30pm*
